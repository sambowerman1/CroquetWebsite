Weekly Recap
Sunday, July 21, 2024
Saturday Debuts and Controversy:
This Saturday marked the much-anticipated debuts of Colin Nuttall and Matthew Kramer at Roslyn National. The games were held at the new satellite course on Lincoln Street, a venue that would later stir some controversy. Kramer clinched the victory in the first game, showcasing his skill and determination. However, the momentum shifted in the second game as Colin seized control. Midway through this game, Kramer made a surprising exit, citing the course conditions as a factor, and chose not to participate in the final game. Colin took advantage of Kramer’s absence and secured victories in the remaining two games, propelling himself to the top of the rankings.

Sunday Showdowns:
Sunday brought a new set of matches at Roslyn National, featuring Sam, Max, Trevor, and Brock, with Brock making his debut. The first game saw Sam emerging as the winner, demonstrating his consistent performance. The second game turned into an intense showdown between Sam and Brock. In a thrilling finish, Brock managed to edge out Sam for the win. Notably, Trevor entertained the crowd with his unconventional billiards stroke, adding a touch of humor to the day’s events.

New Mallets in Action:
This weekend, the league also acquired new mallets, which saw their first bit of action. The players’ feedback on the new equipment was generally positive, though some adjustments are still being made to ensure durability.
